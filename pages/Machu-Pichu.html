Machu Picchu

Machu Picchu is an ancient Inca citadel located in the Andes Mountains of Peru. It is a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.

Built in the 15th century, Machu Picchu is renowned for its impressive architectural and engineering feats. The site features intricate stone structures, terraces, and temples that seamlessly blend into the mountainous landscape.

Some interesting facts about Machu Picchu:

  • It was built around 1450 AD and abandoned about 100 years later.
  • The purpose of Machu Picchu remains a mystery, but it is believed to have been a royal estate or a sacred religious site.
  • The site was never discovered by the Spanish conquistadors and remained largely unknown until the early 20th century.
  • Machu Picchu is situated at an altitude of approximately 2,430 meters (7,970 feet) above sea level.

Visiting Machu Picchu is a breathtaking experience, offering stunning views of the Andes Mountains and a glimpse into the fascinating history of the Inca civilization. Tourists can reach the site by hiking the Inca Trail or taking a train from the nearby town of Aguas Calientes.
